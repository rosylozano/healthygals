Menstruation Cycle:

Another word for menstruation is "period". It is a hormonal process a womens body goes through each month to prepare for a possible pregnancy. It starts on the first day of your last period and ends on the first day of your next period.

Normal Period:

Menstrual flow might occur every 21 to 35 days and last 2 to 7 days. First few years after menstruation begins, longer cycles are common, but start to shorten when you start to age. Within a broad range, "normal" is what's normal for you.The only true normal is what's normal for you.

Age you usually get your period:

Girls usually get their period around the age of 12 to 14 years old, but it can happen years before or years after. If you do not get your period by the age of 15 or when your breasts started to grow talk to your parents/guardians or doctor.

What causes your period?

Natural body chemicals sometimes even your hormones cause your ovaries to release one egg about once a month.

What causes menstrual cycle irregularities?

1. Pregnancy or breastfeeding.

A missed period can be an early sign of pregnancy. Breastfeeding typically delays the return of menstruation after pregnancy.

2. Eating disorders, extreme weight loss or excessive exercising.

Eating disorders such as anorexia nervosa extreme weight loss and increased physical activity can disrupt menstruation.

3. Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).

Women with this common endocrine system disorder may have irregular periods as well as enlarged ovaries that contain small collections of fluid called follicles located in each ovary as seen during an ultrasound exam.

4. Premature ovarian failure.

Premature ovarian failure refers to the loss of normal ovarian function before age 40. Women who have premature ovarian failure also known as primary ovarian insufficiency-might have irregular or occasional periods for years.

5. Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID).

This infection of the reproductive organs can cause irregular menstrual bleeding.
